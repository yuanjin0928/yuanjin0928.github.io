Xilinx Family Lines of Products

Virtex family. Virtex-E family was introduced in 1999. After the release of Virtex-E, Xilinx on average releases new generation of Virtex device every two years, and each new generation of device is manufactured on new process technology. Virtex-5 was introduced in 2006. Since Virtex-5, Xilinx changed the logic fabric from 4-input LUTs to 6-input LUTs. Vritex-7 was introduced in 2010 on a 28 nm process technology. It is reported to deliver a two-fold system performance improvement at 50 percent lower power compared to previous generation Virtex-6 devices. In 2011, Xilinx began shipping sample quantities of the Virtex-7 2000T FPGA which adopts stacked silicon interconnect (SSI) technology. In 2012, using the same 3D technology, Xilinx introduced initial shipments of Virtex-7 H580T FPGA, a heterogeneous device. It comprises two FPGA dies and on 8-channel 28 Gbit/s transceiver die in the same package. With the introduction of its 28nm FPGAs, Xilinx replaced the high-volume Spartan family with the Kintex family and the low-cost Artix family. Kintex family. Artix family. Zynq family. Zynq-7000 integrate a complete ARM Cortex-A9 MPCore-processor-based 28nm system. The Zynq architecture differs from previous marriages of programmable logic and embedded processors by moving from an FPGA-centric platform to a processor-centric model. In 2013, Xilinx introduced the Zynq-7100, which integrates digital signal processing (DSP) to meet emerging programmable systems integration requirements of wireless, broadcast, medical and military applications.
